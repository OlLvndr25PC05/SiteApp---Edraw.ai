10 Practical Kanban Examples

Kanban boards are versatile and serve various industries. Here are 10 examples of Kanban templates available in Edraw.AI, EdrawMax, and EdrawMind’s template community:

Kanban Board for OneNote

This Kanban Board for OneNote helps teams visualize workflows while embracing Kanban methodology. It replaces traditional cards with sticky notes or icons on a shared board.

Divided into sections like To Do, In Progress, and Done, it’s ideal for tracking tasks. It can check production stages or team activities within OneNote’s collaborative interface.

Simple Kanban Board

The Simple Kanban Board is a straightforward way to track tasks using physical boards and sticky notes. Divided into vertical columns, it shows progress as notes move through the workflow.

The template is ideal for quick setups and team demonstrations. The method fosters collaboration but might not suit smaller groups or those preferring digital tools.

Team Kanban Board

The Team Kanban Board is perfect for managing agile projects and keeping teams aligned. It uses columns and cards to track stages and tasks, encouraging collaboration and continuous improvement.

The example is great for blending with other agile practices like scrum or sprints. It helps teams visualize workloads, limit WIP, and streamline the product development process.

Project Kanban Template

The Project Kanban Template simplifies workflow management with ready-made, customizable boards. You can set up stages tailored to your team’s needs with a few clicks. These templates often include helpful features like reminders and work-in-progress limits, making it easier to keep tasks organized and workflows efficient right from the start.

Printable Kanban Board

The Printable Kanban Board offers a hands-on way to manage tasks using simple templates. With columns like To Do, Doing, and Done, it’s easy to visualize workflows and limit WIP.

The board is perfect for practicing Kanban. These boards streamline processes and can be customized for personal or team use, making them a versatile project management tool.

Kanban Template for Excel

The Kanban Template for Excel makes it easy to manage your tasks without needing extra software.

Simply enter your data into the spreadsheet, and with a click, refresh the workbook to update your board. You can customize the board statuses, add multiple boards, and display them separately or together. It’s a flexible, simple way to track workflows right from Excel.

Kanban Board With Business Strategy

The Kanban Board with Business Strategy is all about optimizing workflows and boosting efficiency. It uses cards, columns, and WIP limits to visualize each stage of your process.

By limiting WIP, teams can focus on one task at a time. It improves speed and reduces bottlenecks. It’s a great way to pinpoint workflow issues and improve overall productivity.

Kanban Board for Students

The Kanban Board for Students is a creative and engaging way to teach the writing process. It lets students track their progress through each step, from brainstorming to the final draft.

By moving tasks across the board, students gain a sense of accomplishment. Meanwhile, teachers can track and encourage progress. It's a great way to inspire creativity and boost productivity in the classroom.

Kanban Board Cards

Kanban Board Cards are key components of the Kanban method. It helps visualize individual work items. Each card represents a task as it moves through stages like To Do, In Progress, and Done.

These cards help limit WIP and ensure focus on current tasks. Whether physical sticky notes or digital cards, they streamline workflows and boost efficiency.

Kanban Board With Swimlanes

The Kanban Board with Swimlanes adds extra organization by dividing the board into horizontal lanes. It helps teams track tasks that share similar processes or are linked to specific products or teams.

These swimlanes make it easy to spot bottlenecks and work dependencies. It ensures smoother workflows. They’re flexible and show their value in keeping tasks organized and work flowing.

Tips for Effective Kanban Board Management

A well-managed Kanban board keeps your workflow streamlined and efficient. You can make the most out of this powerful tool by following a few simple practices. Stay organized and productive with these tips:

  • Keep columns simple and clearly defined: Avoid overcomplicating your board. Stick to essential columns like To Do, In Progress, and Done for easy navigation.
  • Regularly review and update tasks: Ensure tasks are progressing and remove outdated ones. Frequent updates keep the board relevant and reduce clutter.
  • Use visual markers for priorities: Add colors, icons, or tags to highlight urgent tasks. This makes it easier for teams to focus on what matters most.
  • Limit tasks in “In Progress” to prevent overload: Set Work-In-Progress (WIP) limits. It ensures tasks are completed before adding new ones, improving team efficiency.
